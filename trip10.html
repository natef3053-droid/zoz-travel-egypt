The Nomad’s Path

Day 1: Arrival & The Mud Citadel

Enter the Great Oasis and find rest in your sanctuary. As the sun begins its descent, ascend the Shali Fortress—a mud-brick marvel where you will witness a sunset that paints the desert in royal gold.

Day 2: Waters of Cleopatra

Experience the magic of the Salt Lakes where you float effortlessly. Seek rejuvenation in the Cleopatra Springs and explore the Mountain of the Dead, home to ancient tombs carved into the rock.

Day 3: The King’s Oracle

Walk the path of Alexander the Great to the Temple of the Oracle. Later, immerse yourself in the Berber culture within the Amazigh Village, discovering crafts that have survived for centuries.

Day 4: The Great Sand Sea

Embark on a grand desert safari across the towering dunes. Surrender to the vastness of the Sahara before we begin our journey back, leaving a piece of your soul in the sands of Siwa.

THE SACRED PAPYRUS

Can I swim in the Salt Lakes?

Absolutely. The celestial waters of the Salt Lakes are famous for their high buoyancy and healing properties. You will float like a leaf on the water's surface.

How long is the desert crossing?

The voyage to Siwa is a true expedition, taking approximately 8 to 10 hours depending on your starting point. You will travel in modern, climate-controlled chariots for maximum serenity.

What is the best time for the Odyssey?

To avoid the scorching breath of the sun, we advise traveling between October and April, when the desert air is cool and the stars shine brightest.
