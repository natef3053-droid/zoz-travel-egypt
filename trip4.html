The Pharaoh’s Path

Awakening & Departure

Your journey begins as our representative collects you from your hotel in Hurghada. Set forth as we gather the seekers of history to begin the voyage to the golden city of Luxor.

Safe Passage

A brief pause at the official security checkpoint ensures a protected and serene crossing through the ancient desert routes.

The Nomad’s Rest

A moment of refreshment at the Kilo 85 oasis. Rest and prepare your spirit for the wonders that have stood for three thousand years.

The Great Karnak Complex

Arrive at the gates of Thebes. Enter the magnificent Karnak Temple, a labyrinth of massive pillars and sacred carvings that once served as the heart of the empire.

Royal Feast

Savor a majestic lunch at a local restaurant, replenishing your energy with the authentic flavors of Upper Egypt before crossing to the West Bank.

The Sentinels of Stone

Stand before the Colossi of Memnon—two towering statues of Pharaoh Amenhotep III that have guarded the necropolis since time immemorial.

The Hidden Valley

Descend into the Valley of the Kings, the eternal resting place of Pharaohs and nobles, where secrets and golden treasures were hidden deep within the mountain.

Temple of the Sun Queen

Witness the architectural masterpiece of Queen Hatshepsut, a mortuary temple carved directly into the limestone cliffs of Deir el-Bahari.

Return to the Shores

As the sun begins its descent, relax on your comfortable voyage back to Hurghada, arriving with the soul-stirring memories of Luxor’s eternal glory.

THE SACRED PAPYRUS

Is the path flexible?

While our core sites are scheduled for maximum wonder, we allow time for personal enjoyment. Our guides can often honor reasonable requests that fit the celestial schedule.

How long is the voyage across the sands?

The journey from Hurghada to Luxor spans approximately 4.5 to 5 hours each way. You will travel in air-conditioned comfort, with a restful stop in between.

What should a traveler carry?

We advise carrying sunblock, a neck pillow for the journey, a hat, sunglasses, and sturdy footwear for exploring the temples and tombs.
